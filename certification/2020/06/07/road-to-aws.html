    Road to AWS certifications

    07 Jun 2020 » certification

    When I first decided to get an AWS certification, I was determined to succeed. However, I quickly felt cautious by the vast array of services and study materials available. Important to note that I didn’t find the tests easy.

    To navigate this challenge, I turned to forums and subreddits to find the most effective study strategies. Since I was personally funding my certification journey and had only one chance to pass the exams, I placed great emphasis on my preparation.

    During this process, I discovered that the best approach was to take practice tests and create written annotations with responses to frequently asked questions. This method resembled how I had studied for my driving license.

    Interestingly, hands-on deployment or practice, such as deploying images to EC2 or creating diagrams in CloudFormation, was not necessary. Instead, I opted for a high-quality, affordable education platform that offered practice tests emulating the duration and difficulty of the exams. I chose Whizlabs Online Certification Training Courses for AWS Exams (no sponsorship).

    Once I consistently scored above the passing mark of 75% on these practice tests, I scheduled my exams. Although I should have aimed for a perfect score, I believed that 80% would suffice.

    It took me one month of study from September 15th to October 15th, 2020, to achieve the Cloud Practitioner level. Another month of study from October 15th to November 15th, 2020, led me to the Solutions Architect level. I dedicated one more month, from November 15th to December 15th, 2020, for the Professional level. Unfortunately, I fell short of the minimum passing score by 69% and they asked 75%.

    The percentage grade is balanced based on the performance of all test takers to assess the level of difficulty. Therefore, while I would have felt proud to have the green checkmark of a specialist, the pass or fail outcome of the exams is statistically determined, which can leave a sour taste. An exam with easy questions can result in low scores if everyone performs well on the difficult questions. Conversely, a challenging exam format can lead to higher scores. Surprisingly, I performed well in tests I thought I wouldn’t pass, particularly the first two.

    Given the extra time I had during the 2020 pandemic, I was able to dedicate myself fully to this endeavor. If I had been working at the time, I believe I would have needed at least six months of preparation for each test.

    In the end, I decided not to pursue this path further. It became a test of my capabilities. However, this experience enhanced my ability to interpret technical texts and focus on tricky contextual questions, as this American-style exam format requires precise answers.

    Obtaining AWS certifications served as a complement to my practical knowledge in Google Cloud. While I was initially intrigued by the market, I now understand that these certifications require ongoing renewal, which may not align with someone who is not directly employed in an AWS infrastructure role. Therefore, I would only consider retaking these tests if I were sponsored and convinced that the investment would be worthwhile.
