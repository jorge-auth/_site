    MTI, Lisbon IOT Week 2015

    07 Jun 2015 » event

    In 2015, the UAlg research team attended the IOT Week in Lisbon to showcase our Microgrid Manager prototype. We created a miniature Microgrid panel at our booth, complete with sensor-equipped plugs and an aggregator computer for scheduling:

    Visitors were drawn to the system’s potential for commercial applications. We demonstrated how the technology could regulate energy consumption and showed live on/off status using 6lowpan electrical plugs.

    The project also capable of adapting the system to communicate with HVAC and Modbus systems which was not demonstrated. Me and the team leader on the right, bellow:

    Close to our booth, we met a researcher from Sweden with a similar system that used a mobile app for device interaction. In contrast, our system was autonomous and made decisions for optimal energy saving based on renewable and load consumption profiles.

    The talks around the event were about the upcoming revolution in 2020 with a surge in IoT device numbers. Turns out that after 2020, the revolution turned out to be quieter, with sensors often going unnoticed in our day to day. That’s probably the intent of IoT, that great technology becomes invisible.

    There were numerous smart city M2M and H2M applications. Caught my attention the LPWAN network applications, which use citizen bandwidth to enable long-range, low-power data transmission. Despite the limitations of free bandwidth and noise, these networks, paired with dedicated cloud portals and company antennas, proves to be promising for connecting devices over long distances. I stay tuned to see what the new applications will bring for the future.
