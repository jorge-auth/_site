    GDG devfest Lisbon, 2017

    09 Dec 2017 » event

    While browsing meetup.com, I stumbled upon the GDG DevFest Lisbon. The description promised a day of connecting with other developers.

    I entered a packed auditorium where most of the talks I attended were about artificial intelligence. At the time, TensorFlow was the trending standard library for A.I.

    The main talk focused on the ethical consequences of artificial intelligence. The moderator was a blogger & Software Engineer, and there were three debaters: a philosopher, a lawyer, and a software engineer.

    The most remarkable thing I remember was a question from an audience member — a Portuguese A.I. researcher who worked in China and had traveled to the event. He said something similar to this:

    """
Nowadays most of the apps and web applications are quite simple...(enumerate some)... They all consist of rows of input forms.
Artificial intelligence is a far more complex field.
"""

    That comment can be considered brash, but it impacted the way I see technology. Indeed, it’s correct that most frontends are like that, although the backend is a different story. I would go further and actually say that most of the successfull applications are basically a search box.

    At least the main premise of the internet is “how to find the information”
