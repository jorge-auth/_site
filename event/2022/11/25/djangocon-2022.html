    DjangoCon Europe 2022

    25 Nov 2022 » event

    DjangoCon 2022, the premier conference for Django, brought together developers from around the world to experience the Django community.

    Held in the city of Porto, the event served as a platform for knowledge sharing, networking, and exploring the latest advancements in Django and web development.

    The conference covered a wide range of topics, including engaging talks, discussions on libraries, scaling applications, adding security, hidden gems, and the power of Django-Admin.

    During the event, I had the opportunity to attend over 35 talks and workshops spanning two days. The only sessions I missed were the django-girls hands-on coding sessions.

    One interesting observation I made was that talks with more specific titles tended to be less engaging, while those with more obscure titles often turned out to be captivating and filled with practical insights.

    Among the valuable advice I received during DjangoCon was a simple yet powerful mantra:

    ''' Always be typing. '''

    Although I don’t take it literally, to avoid burnout, it lingers in my mind as a reminder to constantly challenge myself and keep up with my coding skills.

    A potential useful discovery at DjangoCon 2022 was the demo of a debugging tool called Kolo for django. This framework integrates with the code editor and captures Django requests, enabling professional debugging of HTTP calls and their visualization.

    Another highlight was the introduction of diatáxis, a framework for technical documentation authoring. It was noted as a plus to various fields of engineering, including computer science. Compared to a good cookbook.
