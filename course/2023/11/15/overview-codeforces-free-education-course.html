    Overview of codeforces free education course

    15 Nov 2023 » course

    Thoughts and considerations after completing the free education course on Codeforces. About algorithms and data structures, it is comprised of topics such as suffix arrays, segment trees, binary search, sets unions, and the two-pointers method.

    I started coding in C++ in my part-time, diving briefly into the world of competitive programming.

    There is a laziness that might happen while coding in Python, which is my professional language of choice. Too many libraries that solve difficult problems lead to that. Although not the fault of the language, I think it’s not okay to develop O(n) 1-for cycle or O(n^2) 2-for cycle time complexity solutions.

    I spent a month strengthening the fundamentals. Previously, in my master’s thesis, I worked with genetic algorithms. I studied graphs, linked lists, double-linked lists, and tree structures during my university years. Here and there, I am diving into some algorithm study to add it to my toolbelt.

    The direct relation between time complexity and data structures is straightforward. Devising a solution involves partitioning the problem and reducing the time towards the O(log n) curve. And the rest is history.

    With Christmas 2023 approaching, I will continue solving algorithm and data structures challenges. It’s not the same as building a product, but it improves my critical thinking and problem solving.
