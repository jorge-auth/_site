    Diy workshop, converting an ATX power source into a bench power supply

    07 Jun 2012 » workshop

    Fresh in the world of electronics, with shaky soldering skills, I attended the workshop at the EEE lab to enhance my practical knowledge. In my opinion, this project of transforming an ATX power supply into an electronics utility serves as a great entry point. That’s precisely why I was there – to learn the basics.

    The project consists of separating the cables and soldering them to banana connectors, paying attention to the supply voltages of the ATX, which include +12V, +5V, +3.3V, GND, -3.3V, -5V, and -12V. To achieve a differential of 24V, the trick lies in combining the -12V with the 12V.

    During the workshop, we effectively interconnected all the grounds with a shunt and obtained positive and negative supplies.
