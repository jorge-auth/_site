    What do you do? - 2024 version

    16 Nov 2023 » course

    I get this question a lot. People ask my profession, and I state it clearly: I am a Software Engineer. There are layers of comprehension depending on the level education. Profiles of the listener: 10 years older, high-schooler or tech-savy person.

    People may follow with “what do you really do?”.

    A teacher from uni once warned.

    Guys, when you go to the outside world always sell yourselves as information systems builders. 
Websites are at the reach of normies.
With static site generators, like wordpress, you have little work to develop a functional and pretty website. 
Anybody can do that using Google.

    Its easy to understand why people lack birdseye view over the field. the first mislead is that they may think there is only human to machine interactions.

    There is a plethora of areas within computer science. The difficulty is to just find one.

    I worked for R&D, startups, the energy market, telecommunications, railways, insurance, startups, security and B2B. My skill is one of the most flexible and transversal skills that there is.

    In IoT and network, there is a term called machine-to-machine (M2M). Computers also speak to each other. Thats the missing link that people often forget. Computer science is not about a person in front of the screen. Whats behind the jungle of cables and machines is much more that people dare to imagine.

    There is a world to be explored. There was the world of the app-wonder prodigies that boomed in the last decade from year 2010. Then it stagnate with people claiming back control of their focus by narrowing their favourite apps to a select number of choices. The market changed to a service oriented type of market. the Saas model where behind the curtains people mine data and sell a service company to company or company to person. Nowadays there is a transition to a new rush with the democratization of A.I. so A.I is a growing concept like the apps where years before. A personal genie in the pocket that has integration capabilities. For more depth explained in the book “Superintelligence” from Bostrom.

    My broad definition of what I do is:

    I build information systems solutions that support business. 
Bring services to the cloud. 
Automate tasks that otherwise would lead companies to time expensive processes 
and workers to minslessly press a button a thousand times.

    However, some people often persist with the corkscrew approach, asking, ‘Yes, Clive, that sounds good, but what do you really do?’. THis is when we fall into the scope of the tech-savy explanation.

    I provide a value, that is an intelectual product in the form of code.

    when a company lacks in-house resources to accelerate the materialization of a business idea, I add value usually as part of a team that develops the missing components of a project.

    Examples of what I deliver that fit into the tech-savy profile explanation:

    • Packages. Ready to production API prototypes. Applications ready to deliver to google App engine.

    • Batch work. Testing or scripting. Dispatch the workload of a specific listing of requisites. This usually due to the company lack of expertise or alocation of resources.

    • keys-in-hand CI/CD writen as infrastructure as code that allows the development team to later push work to dev or prod by clicking a button.

    • In-house contractor for companies that require presential support to develop and maintain enterprise software tools/solutions.

    • Stewardship when required to look at logs and detect anomalies during a short period of time.

    In essence, I may accelerate backend prototypes; reduce in-queue workload and help to reach hassle free status. I contribute with my knowledge of backend technologies and Linux operating systems, along with an understanding of cloud platforms like AWS and Google Cloud.

    Keywords: backend; API; automation; cloud; testing; monitoring.
